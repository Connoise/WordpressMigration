About

With over a decade of experience in geographic information systems, I transform spatial data into actionable insights and compelling visual narratives. My practice combines rigorous analytical methodology with creative cartographic design, bridging the technical precision of GIS with the art of effective communication.

Professional Background

My foundation in geographic thinking began with a Bachelor of Arts in Geography from the University of Hawaii at Manoa, where I developed a deep appreciation for spatial relationships and environmental systems. This academic grounding in geographic theory and methodology has proven invaluable throughout my decade-long professional journey.

My GIS practice spans traditional spatial analysis to cutting-edge AI-enhanced workflows. I believe that geographic data tells stories—about communities, environments, and the complex relationships that shape our world. My role is to listen to that data carefully and translate its patterns into maps and analyses that inform decisions and inspire understanding.

Through years of production work across both private and government sectors, I've developed an intuitive sense for data quality, spatial relationships, and the subtle details that separate good analysis from great insights. My experience with government planning systems has given me deep insight into regulatory frameworks, public sector workflows, and the unique challenges of municipal and regional planning processes. Every project becomes an opportunity to reveal hidden connections and make the invisible visible.

Analytical Philosophy

Geography is fundamentally about relationships—between places, people, and processes. My analytical approach prioritizes understanding these connections over simply processing data. I design workflows that not only produce results efficiently but also maintain analytical integrity and reproducibility.

Every map is an argument, every analysis a perspective. I'm committed to creating work that is both technically sound and ethically responsible, recognizing that GIS outputs influence real-world decisions affecting communities and environments.

Value Proposition

In an era of big data and automated analysis, the human geographer's role becomes more crucial, not less. I bring contextual understanding, methodological rigor, and creative problem-solving to spatial challenges. My decade of experience means I can quickly identify the right analytical approach, anticipate potential pitfalls, and deliver results that stakeholders can trust and act upon.

Whether developing custom workflows for complex spatial problems or producing publication-ready cartography, I combine technical expertise with geographic insight to transform data into knowledge.

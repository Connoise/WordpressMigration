About the MicroMashii Series:

MicroMashii are a light personal take on the Plunderponics genre. The project initially referenced music edited within unavoidable limitations: Curb-side PC parts, Longs CVS earbuds, no cables\midi. These limitations combined with the creative wellspring of the golden age of shitposting created the technical template of the MicroMashii. There are no ‘meanings’ to the MicroMashii projects but the overall impression that should be taken is that each piece exists as an experiment in digital technique, composition, and fun. 



Mixes are created digitally with a limited use of traditional syncing techniques. The results create a base field of de-fazed swirling sample-scapes. From this point any direction of composition can be used to further develop the piece. Initially, the lack of computational resources made the stacking of digital compositional elements unpredictably unstable. The traditional ‘glue’ of compression and reverb are at times absent/cranked up for this reason. The sonic environment exists as a collage of unblended extremes.

Samples are cut and selected with little regard to digital source qualities. The clashes of maximalist/minimalist signatures lead to an active argument of tones; fever dream earthquake bumps, solemn peat moss jams. ‘Digital source qualities’ also refer to the more concrete publishing source of each sample.  Some samples are manipulated beyond recognition and some are copy-pasted vanilla and sample sources are never cited. Production-wise this means that the MicroMashii will never be sold. I will remove samples if requested. This is a personal project I make no money but I will always respect individual wishes



These bold limitations allow for the elements of digital manipulation to be picked apart piece-wise. It’s an extremely simple technique that has shaped my signature sound. MicroMashii exists as the accessible fun of digital music making.
